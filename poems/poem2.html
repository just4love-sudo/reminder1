The Space Between Calls

It’s not really your silence that’s breaking me. It’s what’s in between those damn gaps between our calls where I sit and overthink and remember what your voice used to sound like when it still felt warm. You’re not here. But somehow, I still catch you in echoes a dumb joke, a sound that’s a little too close to your laugh, and suddenly you’re everywhere. Your humor? God, it’s stupid. Not even funny half the time. But it still makes me laugh because it’s you. You don’t have to say you care. You never did. You’re there even in the nothingness, the stillness, the space where other people would've already walked away. You were spring in a life that felt like endless grey. And even though my brain forgets everything lately appointments, passwords, people. it doesn’t forget you. Your kindness is burned into me. You’re strongest when you stop pretending. When you cry and don’t hide it. When you’re fragile and don’t say “I’m fine.” You keep yourself locked up, like you’re too much for the world. But I’ve seen all the parts you hide and loved every one of them. Now I just sit in this quiet and try to convince myself that you still think of me when the world slows down. Because honestly, I don’t know how to be okay in the space between calls.
